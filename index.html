My research focuses on purpose, stakeholder identification, and the dynamics of stakeholder theory in crisis situations. It is primarily phenomenon-driven, and I employ qualitative methodologies such as case study analysis and the grounded theory approach.

I have published a paper on Creating Shared Value in the International Journal of Management Reviews, which was Wiley’s top-cited and most downloaded article of the year (2021–2022). Additionally, I received the Best PhD Student Paper Award at the World Open Innovation Conference 2019 and the People’s Choice Award at the Postdoc Symposium 2024 at the University of Virginia.

I hold a Ph.D. in Management from Luiss Guido Carli University, during which I was a visiting Ph.D. researcher at Boston College. I also earned a master’s degree in Business Management from La Sapienza University in Rome and completed an Erasmus program at Södertörn University in Stockholm.

I am passionate about advancing scholarship and practice in organizational purpose and Stakeholder Theory.
